While the CAD and STL files were completed, lack of availability of resources,
such as the 3D Factory's 3D printers and laser cutters, meant that not all
components could be manufactured within the project's timeframe.

## Skull, Jaw and Tongue
As a substitute for the topmost, 3D printed components, an animation was produced
in Blender to simulate the movement of the jaw and tongue, shown in _Figure 1_.

<figure>
  <img src="/img/SkullAnimated.gif" width="960" height="540" />
  <figcaption><strong><em>Figure 1</em></strong>: The animation of the Soapomorph's jaw and tongue.</figcaption>
</figure>

Due to time and experience constraints, the jaw and tongue do not move
simultaneously like the actual mechanism inherently would, but it provides a
useful demonstration of the mechanism's movement and the detachable lid. It also
provides a preview of the visibility of the join, where the separately printed
back and front of the skull meet.

## Base
The lack of availability of laser cutting facilities was accounted for by
assembling a base from parts cut from MDF and plywood, as shown in _Figure 2_.

The door opens easily and smoothly, albeit without an ergonomically designed
handle to ease the user in gripping it, and the compartment comfortably fits a
typically sized bottle of hand soap.
